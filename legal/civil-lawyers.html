Common Questions

A legal notice is a formal communication warning a party of impending litigation if demands are not met. While not mandatory for all civil cases, it is legally required in cases involving government bodies, negotiable instruments (cheque bounce), and often leads to settlements without court intervention.
Traditional civil suits can take several years. However, commercial disputes under the Commercial Courts Act are expedited, often resolving in 1-2 years. We always explore arbitration and mediation for faster resolutions.
Specific performance is a court order compelling a party to fulfill their exact obligations under a contract (e.g., transferring a specific property). Damages refer to monetary compensation awarded to cover the financial loss caused by the breach.
